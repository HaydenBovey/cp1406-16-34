Conductors

Here are some of the conductors of the Barrier Reef Orchestra.

    Mark has guest-conducted at the Melbourne Symphony Orchestra, Moscow Symphony Orchestra, Symphony Orchestra of St. Petersburg and the West Australian Symphony to just name a few. He currently servers as the Artistic Director and Principal Conductor at the Zelman Symphony Orchestra, Ballarat Symphony and the Macquarie Philharmonia. He is passionate about the education and training of future conductors and has been a frequent guest teacher for Masters of Conducting students at the Melbourne Conservatorium.

    Richard has maintained a long career as a bassoonist, music educator, conductor and an arranger. He was appointed as Associate Principal Bassoon with the Sydney Symphony Orchestra when he was 20 in 1968, then moving to The Canberra School of Music as a founding member of the Canberra Wind Soloists in 1978. His work as a conductor is well known, particularly in Canberra as he was the Conductor and Musical Director of the Canberra Youth Orchestra from 1980-1990. His work with the Canberra Orchestra had lead to three highly successful European tours and helped with development and much work with the School of Music Orchestra, Canberra Opera, Canberra Philharmonic Society and 13 years as Musical Director of the Llewellyn Choir.

  • Mario Dobernig

    • Mario grew up in a small Austrian Alps village before starting his professional career as a percussionist in Graz, Austria and Helsinki, Finland. He has graduated from the Art University Graz and the Sibelius Academy of Helsinki, Mario also holds a Masters degree in Musicology from the University of Helsinki. He is currently sudying for his PhD at the University of Melbourne. In 2011 Mario conducted Stravinsky's Histoire du Soldat for the Woodend Winter Arts Festival which was broadcasted on the ABC's classic FM radio.
